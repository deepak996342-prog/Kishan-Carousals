Pillar 01 · Positioning
Who you serve +
the outcome you create.
Positioning is the foundation everything else is built on.

Most people describe what they do. Strong brands describe who they serve and the specific result they create. "I help SaaS founders attract inbound leads through LinkedIn content — without paid ads" is a position. "Marketing consultant" is not.

Your positioning determines who stops on your profile, who follows you, and ultimately who buys from you.

Action: Write your positioning in one sentence: "I help [specific person] achieve [specific outcome] using [your method]."

Pillar 02 · Visual Identity
Your photo, banner,
and colours speak first.
Visual identity communicates trust before a word is read.

A professional headshot increases profile views by up to 14×. Your banner is prime billboard real estate — use it to reinforce your positioning, not decorate.

Consistent colours and visual style across your posts create a recognisable "feed signature." When people see your content, they should know it's yours before they read your name. That's the power of visual consistency.

Action: Audit your headshot (lit, direct gaze, professional context) and banner (positioning + one clear CTA or value statement).

Pillar 03 · Content Voice
A distinctive POV
makes you unmistakable.
Your voice is the thing AI can't replicate about you.

Generic advice is everywhere. What people crave is a distinct point of view — someone who takes a stand, challenges conventional wisdom, or brings a fresh lens to a familiar problem.

Your content voice is shaped by your background, your contrarian takes, your lived experience. Don't try to sound like everyone else in your industry. The more distinctly you sound like you, the more memorable — and shareable — your content becomes.

Action: List 3 things you genuinely believe that most people in your industry get wrong. Build posts around those.

Pillar 04 · Consistent Presence
Showing up regularly
builds mental real estate.
Consistency compounds. Sporadic posting doesn't.

Personal branding is a long game. The people who win are not the most talented — they're the ones who show up consistently over months and years.

3 posts per week for 6 months will do more for your brand than 30 posts in a burst followed by silence. Consistency trains the algorithm, trains your audience, and trains you. You get better, faster, and more confident the more reps you put in.

Action: Commit to a specific cadence — 3 posts per week, same days. Build a content calendar 2 weeks ahead. Never miss.

Pillar 05 · Signature Story
Your origin story is
your strongest asset.
The "why behind the what" creates emotional connection.

People don't just buy expertise — they buy the person behind it. Your signature story answers: Why this field? What did you overcome? What do you now know that you wish you'd known earlier?

Share it in your About section. Reference it in posts. Make it part of how you introduce yourself. A well-told origin story creates instant relatability, trust, and differentiation from every other expert with the same credentials.

Action: Write your 150-word signature story. Include: the turning point, the lesson, and who you now help as a result.
