01
The Problem
Trying to speak to everyone means you resonate with no one
Generalists are invisible. Specialists are magnetic.

"I help businesses grow" is forgettable.
"I help service-based founders get their first 10 clients
from LinkedIn without paid ads" is unforgettable.
The second one attracts — the first one disappears into the feed.

The 3-Part Formula
Who + Problem + Outcome
The simplest niche formula that exists

WHO do you help? (specific audience)
PROBLEM — what frustrates them most?
OUTCOME — what transformation do you deliver?

"I help [WHO] solve [PROBLEM] so they can [OUTCOME]."
That's your niche. That's your content. That's your brand.

03
The Fear
"What if I turn away clients who don't fit?"
That's not a bug. That's the feature.

Clients who don't fit drain your energy and dilute your positioning.
The right clients — attracted by your niche — pay more,
stay longer, and refer others just like them.
Saying no to the wrong fit is how you get paid by the right fit.

04
The Method
Niche on content. Not on services.
You can serve anyone — but speak to one person

You don't have to refuse clients outside your niche.
You just create content that speaks to one specific person.
That person refers others like them.
Content niching is positioning — not a legal contract.
Be known for one thing. Do whatever you want.

05
The Test
Ask: would my ideal client stop scrolling at this post?
If yes — you've found your niche content

Write a post. Then ask: "Would [specific person] read this
and think 'this is exactly what I needed'?"
If the answer is yes — post it.
If it would work for anyone, it will work for no one.
Precision is the point.
