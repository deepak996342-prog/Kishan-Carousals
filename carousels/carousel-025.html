01
Tactic 01
Optimize your profile
before posting anything.
First impressions happen in 3 seconds.

When someone visits your profile, they're making a split-second decision: follow or leave. A weak headline, a generic About section, or a blank banner makes them leave.
Nail your headline (Formula 01), write a client-first About section, and put a clear banner that states your offer. Do this before you post a single word.

02
Tactic 02
Comment on 10 posts
every single day.
Commenting is the fastest follower growth hack.

Every meaningful comment puts your name in front of the poster's entire audience. 10 thoughtful comments per day = 70 new audience touchpoints per week.
Don't say "Great post!" Say something that proves you read it, adds a point, or asks a sharp question. Those comments get replies, likes, and profile visits.

10
comments/day = 300 audience touchpoints per month. Compounding.

03
Tactic 03
Connect with 20 ideal
clients every week.
Not random people. Your exact target audience.

Search for your ideal client — by job title, company size, industry, or keywords. Send a connection request with a 1-sentence personalised note. No pitch. Just a genuine reason to connect.
"I saw your comment on [X]. Your point about [Y] resonated — connecting." That's it. Most people accept. Now you're in each other's feeds.

04
Tactic 04
Post 4–5 times
per week. Consistently.
Volume beats perfection at the start.

The first 1,000 followers is a volume game. You don't know yet which posts will hit. So post consistently and let data tell you what resonates.
Your 10th post will perform better than your 1st. Your 50th better than your 10th. The algorithm rewards creators who show up. Show up before you feel ready.

4×
posts/week over 12 weeks = 48 posts. Your whole content game changes.

05
Tactic 05
Ask your network
to reshare your best post.
One repost from the right person = 500 new eyeballs.

When you write a post you're proud of, message 5 connections who'd find it genuinely useful. "Hey [name], wrote something that might be useful for your audience — if you agree, a reshare would mean a lot."
This is not spammy. It's how real creators grow. Most people are happy to help if you ask directly and sincerely.
