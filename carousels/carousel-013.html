01
Phase 01 — Minutes 1–3
3 min
Write your hook — 3 lines only

Line 1: The bold claim or uncomfortable truth.
Line 2: The tension or contradiction.
Line 3: The promise of what's coming.
3 lines. Under 30 words. That's it.
If you nail the hook, 80% of the work is done.

02
Phase 02 — Minutes 4–12
8 min
Brain-dump the body — no editing

Write everything you know about this topic.
Don't edit. Don't delete. Don't judge.
You're in "create mode" not "edit mode."
Aim for 200–400 words. You'll cut later.
Fast drafts beat perfect drafts every time.

03
Phase 03 — Minutes 13–17
5 min
Edit ruthlessly — cut everything weak

Read it once. Delete anything that isn't essential.
Cut adverbs, filler phrases, and humble-brags.
Break long sentences. Add white space.
Read it out loud — if you stumble, rewrite it.
Target: every line earns its place.

04
Phase 04 — Minutes 18–20
2 min
Add the CTA and a question

Every post needs one clear next step.
"If this resonated, share it with someone who needs it."
"Drop a comment — I read every one."
"DM me 'AUDIT' for a free content review."
Pick one. One CTA. Never two.

The Trap to Avoid
Over-editing
Perfection is procrastination in disguise

The post you spent 3 hours on won't outperform
the one you wrote in 20 minutes and just posted.
A published post beats a perfect draft — every time.
Your audience rewards consistency, not perfection.
Set a timer. When it rings, post it.
