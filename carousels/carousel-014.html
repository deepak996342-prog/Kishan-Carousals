01
Step 01 — Before You DM
Warm them up with 3 real comments first

A DM from a stranger is cold. A DM from someone
who's commented on your posts twice is warm.
Before you DM anyone: comment genuinely on
3 of their posts over 3–5 days. Now you're familiar.

02
Step 02 — The First DM
Reference something specific. No ask.

No pitch. No "I can help you." Just a genuine connection.

Example
"Hey [Name], I saw your post about [X] — really resonated. I work with [similar audience] and ran into the same thing. Thought it was worth reaching out."

03
Step 03 — The Conversation
Ask 1 question. Listen. Ask another.

Don't info-dump your offer. Ask about their world.
"What's the biggest challenge you're facing with [X] right now?"
Then actually listen. Ask a follow-up question.
Two exchanges of real conversation builds more trust
than 10 bullet points about your services.

04
Step 04 — The Soft Offer
Mention the help only if it naturally fits

Only after they've shared their problem do you mention a solution.
Never before.

Example
"I actually help [their type of business] with exactly that. Would it be useful to jump on a quick 30-minute call? No pitch — just want to share what's worked."

✕
What Kills the DM
3 things that instantly end the conversation

1. Pitching in the first message. Nobody asked.
2. Copy-paste templates. People can feel it immediately.
3. "I came across your profile and…" — the most recognisable cold DM opener. Delete it forever.

Be specific. Be real. Be genuinely curious about them.
