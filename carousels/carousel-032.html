Phase 01 · The First Hour
Your post gets tested
on a small audience first.
The algorithm runs a 60-minute experiment on every post.

When you publish, LinkedIn shows your post to a small slice of your connections — roughly 5–10%. It measures how they engage. High comments and reposts in the first hour = wide distribution. Low engagement = post suppressed.
This is why the first 60 minutes are critical. Be present. Reply to every comment. The algorithm reads your replies as additional engagement signals.

Phase 02 · Signal Hierarchy
Not all engagement
signals are equal.
Comments > Reposts > Reactions > Views.

LinkedIn weights engagement signals differently. Comments are the strongest signal. They indicate real attention. Reposts indicate strong value. Reactions are weak. Views barely count alone.
A post with 8 thoughtful comments will reach further than one with 80 likes. Engineer comments by asking questions, taking positions that invite response, and replying to keep threads alive.

Hack: Ask a direct question at the end of every post. "What would you add?" generates 3× more comments than no CTA.

Phase 03 · Dwell Time
The algorithm measures
how long people read.
Dwell time is a massive hidden ranking signal.

LinkedIn tracks how long someone pauses on your post before scrolling. Long-form text posts and carousels naturally create high dwell time. Videos require autoplay and sound-off compatibility to hold attention.
Write posts that take 30–60 seconds to read. Use line breaks to increase scroll time. Every second someone spends reading sends a positive signal.

Phase 04 · Relevance Score
The algorithm decides
who sees your post.
It shows your content to people most likely to engage.

LinkedIn matches your post to people who have engaged with similar content before. If your audience engages consistently, the algorithm learns to show your content to more people like them.
This means consistency trains the algorithm. The more consistently you post and engage, the more precisely LinkedIn knows your audience — and the better it places your content.

What Kills Reach
3 things that suppress
your post immediately.
Avoid these — they signal low-quality content.

1. External links in the post body. LinkedIn wants users to stay on platform. Links out = suppressed reach. Put links in the first comment instead.
2. Posting and disappearing. If you don't engage in your own comment section, the algorithm reads this as low-value content.
3. Buying likes or using engagement pods. LinkedIn's AI detects inauthentic patterns and quietly reduces organic reach.
