Foundation · Pillar Content
Start with one deep,
comprehensive piece.
Everything else is derived from this single source of truth.

A pillar piece is your long-form anchor — a 2,000-word article, a 60-minute interview, a detailed case study, or an in-depth newsletter issue. It's the complete, expert-level treatment of a topic you know deeply.

This is not a LinkedIn post. It's the source material from which all your LinkedIn posts will be pulled. Think of it as the diamond — everything else you create is cut from it.

Pillar Ideas: A client transformation story · Your complete framework · A step-by-step process · A contrarian take with full evidence

Format 01 · Carousel
Break it into
a visual slide deck.
Carousels get 3× more reach than text posts — and save your content.

Take the key framework, process, or list from your pillar content and turn it into a carousel. Each slide = one idea. 8–12 slides is the sweet spot.

A carousel forces you to distil your thinking into clear, scannable visuals. The algorithm loves them because they generate high dwell time and swipe interactions — both of which signal quality content to LinkedIn's distribution engine.

One pillar piece can easily produce 2–3 different carousel angles.

Extraction method: Pull the numbered list, the step-by-step process, or the comparison framework directly from your pillar content.

Format 02 · Text Post Threads
Turn each section into
a standalone post.
A 5-section pillar article = 5 separate LinkedIn posts.

Every major section or insight from your pillar content can become a single LinkedIn text post. Reframe the angle, write a new hook, and let that one idea breathe on its own.

The pillar article on "5 reasons clients leave agencies" becomes 5 posts — one for each reason, each with a unique hook and angle. Posted across 5 weeks, that's an entire month of content from one article.

This is the fastest path to consistency without running out of ideas.

Rule: Change the hook on every post. Same insight, fresh entry point. Your audience sees different angles, not repetition.

Format 03 · Short Video Clips
Record yourself explaining
one insight. 90 seconds.
LinkedIn native video gets priority reach — and builds trust faster than text.

Pick one idea from your pillar content — the most surprising, contrarian, or actionable insight. Record a 60–90 second LinkedIn video where you explain it directly to camera.

No editing required. Phone, natural light, one clear point. Your face builds trust faster than any caption. Video also surfaces to people outside your network, giving you broader organic reach than text alone.

Post the video with the full explanation in the caption for the scrollers who don't watch.

Format: State the insight in 10 seconds. Explain why in 40 seconds. Give the action step in 20 seconds. Done.

Format 04 · Polls + Stories
Turn your insights into
questions for your audience.
Polls generate comments + data. Stories humanise your expertise.

From your pillar content, extract a debatable question and turn it into a LinkedIn poll. "What's the #1 reason content strategies fail?" with 4 options from your article generates comments, data, and reach — while subtly positioning your expertise.

Meanwhile, extract the human story behind the insight — the client struggle, the mistake you made, the realisation moment — and write it as a personal narrative post. Story-driven posts are LinkedIn's highest-engagement format and require zero new ideas.

Repurposing math: 1 pillar piece → 2 carousels + 5 text posts + 2 videos + 2 polls + 1 story = 12 pieces of content.
