Formulas 01–02 · Number + Contrarian
Lead with a number.
Or challenge a belief.
The two most reliable scroll-stoppers in the LinkedIn playbook.

Formula 1 — The Number Hook: Specific numbers trigger curiosity. The brain wants to know what the list contains before committing to reading.

Formula 2 — The Contrarian Hook: Challenge a widely-held belief and you immediately create tension. Tension demands resolution. Resolution requires reading on.

Formula 1 Example
"7 LinkedIn mistakes that cost me 3,000 followers in a single month."
Formula 2 Example
"Posting every day is the worst thing you can do for your LinkedIn growth."

Formulas 03–04 · Question + Bold Claim
Ask the question
they're already asking.
Or make a claim so bold they can't scroll past it without knowing if it's true.

Formula 3 — The Question Hook: Use the exact question your ideal reader is already asking themselves. Mirror their internal dialogue and they feel like you're reading their mind.

Formula 4 — The Bold Claim Hook: State something that sounds impossible or extreme — but that you can back up. The bigger the claim, the higher the scroll-stopping power.

Formula 3 Example
"Why do some LinkedIn creators get 50K views on a simple text post while yours gets 200?"
Formula 4 Example
"I booked 14 clients in 30 days without a single cold outreach message."

Formulas 05–06 · Failure + Result Reveal
Admit the failure.
Or tease the result.
Vulnerability and curiosity gaps are two of the most powerful hooks in existence.

Formula 5 — The Failure Hook: Admitting a public mistake or failure instantly creates relatability and trust. Nobody can resist learning what went wrong — and more importantly, how you recovered.

Formula 6 — The Result Reveal Hook: State the end result first. The reader is immediately asking "how?" — and they have to read to find out.

Formula 5 Example
"I spent £12,000 on LinkedIn ads and got zero clients. Here's what I learned."
Formula 6 Example
"This one carousel post generated £45K in new business. Here's exactly why it worked."

Formulas 07–08 · Time Frame + If-Then
Compress time.
Or create a condition.
Speed creates urgency. Conditional statements create personalisation.

Formula 7 — The Time Frame Hook: Compressing a transformation into a specific timeframe makes results feel concrete and achievable. It also signals efficiency — you'll help them get there faster.

Formula 8 — The If-Then Hook: This hook self-selects your ideal reader. "If you [specific situation]..." immediately filters who reads on — and those who do are highly qualified.

Formula 7 Example
"In 90 days, I went from 800 to 12,000 LinkedIn followers. Here's the exact system."
Formula 8 Example
"If you're posting consistently on LinkedIn but not generating leads, read this."

Formulas 09–10 · List Hook + Confession
Promise a list.
Or confess something real.
Lists promise structured value. Confessions create authentic connection.

Formula 9 — The List Hook: Promising a list creates an open loop. The reader's brain immediately wants to know all the items. It's one of the oldest and most reliable hooks in content history — because it works.

Formula 10 — The Confession Hook: "I used to think X. I was wrong." This opens with intellectual honesty and a reversal. People are wired to follow along when someone publicly changes their mind based on evidence.

Formula 9 Example
"10 things every LinkedIn creator should stop doing immediately (most do 6+ of these)."
Formula 10 Example
"I used to think follower count was everything. I was completely wrong — here's what actually matters."

Quick Reference

10 hook formulas — save this and use it every time you write

01
Number Hook — "[X] things/reasons/mistakes that..."
02
Contrarian — "Everyone says [X]. They're wrong."
03
Question — "Why does [pain point] happen to you but not them?"
04
Bold Claim — "I [extreme result] without [expected method]."
05
Failure — "I [failed publicly]. Here's what I learned."
06
Result Reveal — "[Big result]. Here's exactly how."
07
Time Frame — "In [X days], I went from [A] to [B]."
08
If-Then — "If you [specific situation], read this."
09
List Hook — "[X] things [specific person] must stop doing."
10
Confession — "I used to believe [X]. I was completely wrong."
Rule: Write 5 different hooks before choosing one. The first is almost never the best.
