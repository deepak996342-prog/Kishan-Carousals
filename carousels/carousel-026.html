The Formula
The 3-layer comment
that builds your brand.
Validate → Expand → Invite

Layer 1 — Validate: Acknowledge the post's main point genuinely.
Layer 2 — Expand: Add your own perspective, data point, or experience.
Layer 3 — Invite: Ask a question or make an observation that invites reply.
This structure turns your comment into a mini-post — and it always gets noticed.

Example
"Totally agree. [Expand with your angle.] The part I'd add is [your insight]. Has anyone found that [question to invite replies]?"

Timing Strategy
Comment in the first
30 minutes. Always.
Early comments get 10× the visibility.

LinkedIn's algorithm shows early comments to a much wider audience — before the post even gets significant traction. Being one of the first 5 comments on a post puts your name in the high-visibility window.
Turn on notifications for your target creators. Comment within 20–30 minutes of them posting. Consistency here builds recognition fast.

Your Own Posts
Reply to every comment
within the first hour.
Replies double your post's reach — guaranteed.

Every reply you leave on your own post is another touchpoint in someone's feed. A post with 10 replies has 10× more engagement signal than one with 10 likes.
Reply fast. Reply with substance. Ask follow-up questions. Treat your comment section like a conversation — because it is one, and LinkedIn rewards it heavily.

The Rule
Never let a comment go unanswered for more than an hour on posting day. Set a timer. This alone multiplies reach.

Comment Quality Matrix

Weak vs strong comments — side by side

✕ Weak Comment
"Great post! So inspiring 🔥"
Result: invisible. No profile visits. No follows.
✓ Strong Comment
"The point about consistency is underrated — I'd add that the first 30 days feel like posting to nobody, but week 6 is where everything shifts."
Result: profile visits, new followers, DMs.
✕ Weak Comment
"I agree 100%! Well said."
Result: ignored. No algorithm signal. No authority.
✓ Strong Comment
"Counterpoint: [your take]. In my experience [insight]. Has anyone found [question]?"
Result: liked, replied to, shared — full engagement loop.
Your comments are permanent brand impressions on someone else's audience. Write them like you'd write a post.
