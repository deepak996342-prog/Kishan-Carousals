Section 01 · The Hook
First 2 lines show
before "see more."
These 2 lines do 80% of the work.

Most About sections start with "I am a [title] with X years of experience." That's the worst possible hook. Your reader doesn't care about your history — they care about their own problem.
Lead with a bold claim, a provocative question, or a result your client wants. Make them click "see more" before they've read anything else.

Strong Hook Example
Most founders post consistently and still get zero leads. I help fix that in 90 days.

Section 02 · Who You Serve
Name your ideal client
in the first paragraph.
The right person should feel immediately seen.

If your About section could describe anyone, it will attract no one. Get specific about who you work with, what their situation looks like, and what problem they're stuck on.
When your ideal client reads "this is for someone who's struggling with exactly what I'm struggling with" — they don't just read further. They DM you.

Strong Example
I work with service business owners doing £5K–£20K/month who are posting on LinkedIn but not seeing it convert to clients.

Section 03 · Your Story
Tell the origin story.
Not your CV.
People hire people, not resumes.

The middle of your About section should tell the story of why you do this work — a problem you faced, a moment that shifted your thinking, or the client result that proved your approach worked.
This is where you make the human connection. It's where someone goes from "this looks interesting" to "I want to work with this person."

Story Prompt
"Three years ago, I was in the exact same position as most of my clients: posting every day on LinkedIn and wondering why nothing was happening. Then I discovered..."

Section 04 · Proof
Show results.
Not vague claims.
Specificity is credibility.

Don't say "I help clients get results." Say: "A recent client went from 400 followers and no leads to 3 inbound discovery calls per week in 60 days."
Name the outcome. Name the timeframe. Name the transformation. If you have a client testimonial quote, use it. One specific result beats ten generic claims every time.

Proof Formula
[Client type] went from [before state] to [after state] in [timeframe]. Here's how the process works...

Section 05 · The CTA
End with one
clear next step.
Most About sections just... end. That's a wasted conversion.

After building trust through your hook, audience, story, and proof — tell the reader exactly what to do next. One action. Specific. Easy to take.
"If this sounds like where you are, DM me 'AUDIT' and I'll walk you through what I'd do differently with your content — no cost, no pitch."
That's it. That simple sentence books calls.

CTA Formula
If [condition that matches your ideal client], DM me [word] and [specific next thing that happens].
