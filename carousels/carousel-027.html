Stage 01 · Awareness
They find you through
content or a comment.
The first touchpoint — make it count.

A stranger scrolls past your post or sees your comment on someone else's content. In 3 seconds, they decide: "Is this person worth following?"
Your hook, your profile photo, and your first line do the work here. If your content doesn't stop the scroll, the journey ends before it begins.

Goal at this stage: earn the follow. Nothing else. Don't pitch. Don't sell. Just deliver value that makes "follow" feel obvious.

Stage 02 · Trust Building
They read 4–6 posts.
They start to believe you.
Trust is built through repeated, consistent value.

After following, the new connection sees your content for 2–3 weeks. Each post either builds or erodes trust. Education posts build credibility. Story posts build likeability. Proof posts build believability.
By post 6, they're not just following you — they're thinking of you as an expert in this space.

Goal at this stage: become the person they think of first for your specific topic. Consistency does this automatically.

Stage 03 · Engagement
They comment. They DM.
They raise their hand.
This is the warm lead signal — don't miss it.

When someone comments "I needed this today" or DMs "Can I ask you something?" — that's a buying signal. They're not ready to hire yet, but they're close.
Respond fast. Go deep in the conversation. Ask what problem they're trying to solve. Don't pitch yet — just demonstrate you understand their situation better than anyone.

Goal at this stage: open a real conversation. Every DM is a potential discovery call waiting to happen.

Stage 04 · Soft Offer
Make it easy to take
the next step.
Low-friction CTAs convert better than hard sells.

After 2–3 exchanges, offer a free resource, free audit, or a 20-minute discovery call. Frame it as help, not a pitch. "Based on what you've shared, I think I can help — want to jump on a quick call and I'll show you exactly what I'd do?"
That's not selling. That's diagnosing. It has an extremely high acceptance rate.

Goal at this stage: get on a call. Not to pitch — to understand their problem deeply enough to help or not help.

Stage 05 · Convert
The call. The proposal.
The "yes."
By now, this isn't a sale — it's a formality.

If the journey above was done right, by the time they're on a call with you they already trust you, believe in your expertise, and have raised their hand as someone with the exact problem you solve.
At this point, the close is 80% done. Listen more than you talk. Diagnose before you prescribe. Propose clearly. Follow up once. That's it.

Goal at this stage: confirm fit. If they're a good client, say yes. If not, refer them out. That integrity builds more word-of-mouth than any ad.
