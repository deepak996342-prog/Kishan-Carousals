01
Formula 01
The Counterintuitive
"Everyone says [X]. But [opposite truth]."
Example
"Everyone says post daily. But one great post a week beats seven mediocre ones."

Challenges assumptions. Forces a pause. Makes people want to know why you're right.

02
Formula 02
The Specific Number
"I did [X] for [N] days/clients. Here's what happened."
Example
"I audited 47 founder LinkedIn profiles last month. Only 3 had a content strategy that could actually convert."

Specificity signals credibility. "47" is believable. "many" is not.

03
Formula 03
The Painful Truth
"Nobody wants to hear this, but [hard truth]."
Example
"Nobody wants to hear this, but your content isn't underperforming because of the algorithm. It's because nobody cares about your product yet."

Discomfort creates attention. Readers lean in to confirm or deny.

04
Formula 04
The Before / After
"6 months ago: [bad state]. Today: [transformed state]."
Example
"6 months ago: 200 followers, zero leads, posting into the void. Today: 4,800 followers, 3 inbound calls a week. Same person. Different strategy."

Transformation is irresistible. It sells possibility, not features.

05
Formula 05
The Open Loop
"I [did unusual thing]. Here's what I learned."
Example
"I stopped posting on LinkedIn for 30 days. Then I came back and did this one thing. My reach tripled."

Unresolved tension forces the scroll. The brain needs the answer.

Bonus Rules

3 rules that make every hook land harder

1
Write 10 hooks. Use the best 1. Your first hook is never your best hook. Neither is your second.
2
Make it about them, not you. "I did X" only works if the reader sees themselves in X.
3
Short sentences hit harder. Read it out loud. If you run out of breath — cut it in half.
