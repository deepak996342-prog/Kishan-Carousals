Tier 01 · Priority
Connect with your
ideal clients first.
They're the whole point of your LinkedIn presence.

Every week, send 20 connection requests to people who match your ideal client profile — by job title, company size, industry, or challenge. Don't wait to be discovered. Go to where your clients are.
Use the search filters: "Marketing Directors at SaaS companies with 50–200 employees." Get specific. Send requests with a personal note.

Weekly goal: 20 ideal client connection requests, 3–5 accepted, 1 conversation started.

Tier 02 · Leverage
Build relationships
with referral partners.
People who serve your clients but don't compete with you.

Referral partners are people whose clients are your clients — but they offer something completely different. A web designer who works with the same founders you do. A bookkeeper who serves the same small business owners.
Connect. Support their content. When their client needs what you do, your name comes up. This is the highest-ROI network building activity on LinkedIn.

Identify 10 complementary service providers serving your exact audience. Connect and start genuine conversations.

Tier 03 · Community
Connect with peers
doing similar work.
Your competition is also your community.

Connecting with people who do similar work to you creates an unexpected benefit: they refer overflow clients, they reshare your best content, they introduce you to their network.
Competitors are only competition if you think transactionally. Think collaboratively, and your "competition" becomes one of your most powerful growth levers.

Find 20 peers in your niche. Follow, comment on their content, and connect. Relationship before collaboration.

Tier 04 · Visibility
Engage with creators
your clients follow.
Borrowed authority is still authority.

Find the 10–15 LinkedIn creators your ideal clients already follow. Comment thoughtfully on their posts every single day. Their audience is your audience. By consistently showing up in their comment section with real insight, you become known before you've ever directly connected.
Once you've left 10+ meaningful comments, send a genuine connection request. Most will accept — and now you have direct access to their network.

The Nurture System
Connecting is step one.
Nurturing is the job.
A cold connection is worthless without follow-through.

After connecting, the relationship needs tending. Comment on their content. React to their updates. Send a DM when they share something relevant to you.
No pitch. No ask. Just genuine human interest. The people in your network who matter most will be the ones who feel like you're actually paying attention to them — not just collecting them.

Weekly habit: check in on 5 key connections. React. Comment. Reply. That's all it takes to stay top of mind.
